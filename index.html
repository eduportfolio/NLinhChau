I’m Nguyen Linh Chau, a 12th-grade student at Hanoi-Amsterdam High School for the Gifted. Growing up in a manufacturing family, I realized how the Fed’s seemingly distant rate hikes could profoundly narrow small manufacturers’ profit margins like my father’s. Therefore, I want to learn to manage risks through finance, and use finance to pursue and articulate my initiatives.

Believing that investments should be grounded in environmental, social, and governance (ESG) principles, I’m especially passionate about promoting environmental sustainability. At the same time, I’m deeply interested in education, particularly financial literacy and planning, as I see it as a key to long-term sustainability and empowerment.

With the knowledge and skills I gain in college, I want to develop strategies that enable individuals and communities to make informed as well as socially responsible financial decisions. To me, finance is more of a powerful force for positive social change.

Professional Development

The Development of Internet Banking in Vietnam: Challenges, Solutions and Real Applications

Co-Researcher & Data Analyst | Published Research Paper

Jun 2025 - Sep 2025 5 hrs/week x 12 weeks Published

Publication: International Journal of Management, Social Sciences and Marketing (IJMSM)

This research analyzes the advancement of Internet Banking in Vietnamese commercial banks, identifying infrastructure challenges, cybersecurity risks, and proposing sustainable development solutions through comparative international analysis.

Key Research Contributions
  • Comprehensive Data Collection: Gathered and processed secondary data from 60+ reports including State Bank of Vietnam statistics (2015-2024) and annual reports from 5 major banks (Vietcombank, BIDV, VietinBank, Techcombank, MB Bank)
  • International Comparative Analysis: Conducted quantitative comparison between Vietnam and 4 regional countries (Singapore, Malaysia, South Korea, Thailand) across 15 key indicators
  • Data Visualization: Created 12+ charts and data tables using Excel, SPSS & Power BI to illustrate Internet Banking growth trends, showing 27% average annual growth (2018-2024)
  • Financial Inclusion Analysis: Documented non-cash payment ratio reaching 78.6% by end of 2023, analyzing digital banking infrastructure growth across ASEAN
  • Technology Assessment: Evaluated security frameworks, multi-layer authentication systems, and AI/Big Data applications in Vietnamese digital banking
